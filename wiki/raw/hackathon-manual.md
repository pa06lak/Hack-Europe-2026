## Competition Rules & Submission Guidelines

To compete and have your project considered by the jury, all participants must adhere
to the following rules and submission requirements. Failure to meet these guidelines
may result in disqualification.

### Submission Requirements

To qualify for the final judging, you must:
- **Submit your project by 19:00**
- Be a team of **max. 5 people**
- Use **min. 3 partner technologies** (listed under Resources)
- Have **created your project newly at this hackathon** (boilerplates are allowed)

### What needs to be submitted

**Project Presentation**
- Record a **2-minute video demo** of your project (Loom or equivalent)
- Your presentation must include:
  - Detailed explanation of your solution
  - Demonstration of key features with a **live walkthrough**

**Open Source Repository**
- Provide a **public GitHub repository** containing your project's source code
- Your repository must include:
  - Comprehensive **README** with setup and installation instructions
  - Clear documentation of all APIs, frameworks, and tools utilised
  - Sufficient technical documentation to enable thorough jury evaluation

## Competition Mode

Two-stage competition format, culminating in a live final presentation event.

### Stage 1: Pre-Selection
- Build anything aligned with your creative vision — complete freedom of topic choice
- **5 finalist teams** (4 Open Track Winners, 1 Track Winner) advance to the Finalist Stage
- Judging criteria: **creativity, technical complexity**, with **bonus points for effective use of partner technologies**

### Stage 2: Finalist Stage
- All finalists showcase their projects live before jury and audience
- Each team delivers a **5-minute presentation**
- After all presentations, the jury selects the **top 3 winners**
- These top 5 teams are awarded the Finalist Stage Prizes

## Hackathon Tracks, Side Challenges & Prizes

### Tracks

#### Attio — The Agentic CRM
**Track Prize: iPad per team member**

Challenge: Most CRMs are glorified spreadsheets. Attio is built on a different premise:
a CRM shouldn't just hold context, it should act on it. That's agentic CRM — your customer
data becomes the foundation for agents that work autonomously, around the clock.

What to build: An agent or headless system that automates a meaningful chunk of a sales,
recruiting, or customer workflow — following up on leads, researching contacts, updating
deal status from a call, drafting outreach. North star: can your agent close a deal without
a human in the loop?

Some directions: Proactive Relationship Agent · Outbound Email Agent · Inbound Lead Router ·
Deal Risk Monitor · Research-to-CRM Pipeline.

How to build on Attio (four entry points):
- Workflows — custom workflow blocks with your own triggers and logic (https://docs.attio.com/sdk/workflow-blocks/overview)
- Attio MCP — wire Attio into any MCP-compatible agent (https://docs.attio.com/mcp/overview)
- REST API — full programmatic access to the data model (https://docs.attio.com/rest-api/overview)
- App SDK — deeper integrations and custom surfaces inside Attio (https://docs.attio.com/sdk/overview)

Free Attio workspaces available on the day. Bring your own data or mock it.
